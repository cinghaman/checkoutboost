## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are Shopify Plus merchants, ecommerce operators, and growth teams evaluating checkout tools that can increase average order value without adding operational friction. They are likely comparing app options quickly and want immediate clarity on capabilities, trust, pricing, and ease of setup.

### Brand Personality
Confident, polished, merchant-focused. The interface should feel premium and commercially credible, with enough warmth to make promotional tooling feel approachable rather than technical or heavy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use the existing soft-luxury visual language already present in the site: bright surfaces, tinted neutrals, rounded geometry, and restrained gradients. Avoid harsher SaaS dashboard aesthetics, dark-mode neon treatments, or playful consumer-app styling that would weaken merchant trust.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize conversion clarity over novelty.
2. Keep premium styling, but make interaction states explicit and accessible.
3. Preserve the current visual system and polish within it instead of introducing a new brand direction.
4. Reduce friction on mobile and keyboard navigation first.
5. Treat motion as supportive, and always provide a reduced-motion fallback.
